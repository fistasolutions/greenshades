### Learning Path Recommendations

#### For Developers
1. **Beginner:** AI for Everyone (Coursera)
2. **Intermediate:** AI Programming with Python (Udacity)
3. **Advanced:** Deep Learning Specialization (Coursera)
4. **Practice:** Build projects, contribute to open source

#### For Non-Technical Staff
1. **Beginner:** AI for Everyone (Coursera)
2. **Intermediate:** Google AI Fundamentals
3. **Advanced:** AI in Business courses
4. **Practice:** Use AI tools in daily work

#### For Leadership
1. **Beginner:** AI for Everyone (Coursera)
2. **Intermediate:** AI Business School (Microsoft)
3. **Advanced:** AI Strategy courses
4. **Practice:** Apply to strategic planning

## Try It: Exercise

**Scenario:** You want to learn more about AI for your role.

**Task:** Create a learning plan using external resources. Include:
1. Your role and current skill level
2. Recommended courses (2-3)
3. Additional resources (books, podcasts, channels)
4. Timeline for completion

**Solution (Example for Developer):**
```
External Learning Plan:

1. Role: Developer, Intermediate AI skill level
   - Comfortable with AI tools
   - Want to learn deep learning

2. Recommended Courses:
   - Deep Learning Specialization (Coursera) - 4 months
   - Practical Deep Learning (Fast.ai) - 7 weeks
   - Natural Language Processing (Stanford) - 10 weeks

3. Additional Resources:
   - Books: "Deep Learning" by Goodfellow
   - YouTube: 3Blue1Brown, Sentdex
   - Podcasts: Lex Fridman Podcast
   - News: The Batch newsletter

4. Timeline:
   - Months 1-4: Deep Learning Specialization
   - Months 5-6: Practical Deep Learning
   - Months 7-8: Natural Language Processing
   - Ongoing: Books, YouTube, podcasts
```

## Phrases & Prompts That Work

**When choosing courses:**
- "Start with 'AI for Everyone' for fundamentals, then advance based on your role."
- "Combine courses with practice—build projects to reinforce learning."

**When learning:**
- "Follow structured courses for fundamentals, YouTube for quick tutorials."

- "Apply what you learn to real projects—practice is key."
- "Join communities and forums for help and collaboration."
