## Core Content

### A-C

**Algorithm**
- **Definition:** A set of rules or instructions for solving a problem
- **Plain Language:** The "recipe" that tells a computer how to process data
- **Example:** A sorting algorithm organizes payroll records by employee ID
- **Greenshades Context:** Tax calculation algorithms determine withholding amounts

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**
- **Definition:** AI with human-level intelligence across all cognitive tasks
- **Plain Language:** AI that can do anything a human can do intellectually
- **Status:** Not yet achieved (estimated 10-50 years away)
- **Greenshades Context:** Future goal; we currently use Narrow AI

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
- **Definition:** Machines performing tasks requiring human intelligence
- **Plain Language:** Computers that can understand, learn, and make decisions
- **Example:** ChatGPT understanding questions and generating answers
- **Greenshades Context:** AI tools like GitHub Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT

**Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI)**
- **Definition:** AI specialized for one specific task or domain
- **Plain Language:** AI that's really good at one thing (like playing chess or translating languages)
- **Status:** Current reality (all AI tools today are ANI)
- **Greenshades Context:** All AI tools we use (Copilot, ChatGPT) are Narrow AI

**Attention Mechanism**
- **Definition:** Technique that allows AI models to focus on relevant parts of input
- **Plain Language:** Like highlighting important words when reading a document
- **Example:** When translating "The payroll system processes 10,000 records," the model focuses on "payroll system" and "10,000 records"
- **Greenshades Context:** Enables language models to understand context in payroll documents

**Bias (AI Bias)**
- **Definition:** Systematic errors or unfairness in AI model outputs
- **Plain Language:** AI making incorrect or unfair assumptions based on training data
- **Example:** AI resume screening tool favoring certain demographics
- **Greenshades Context:** Ensuring payroll AI doesn't discriminate based on employee characteristics

**Chatbot**
- **Definition:** AI program that converses with users via text or voice
- **Plain Language:** A computer program you can talk to like a human
- **Example:** Customer service chatbot answering payroll questions
- **Greenshades Context:** Employee support chatbot for payroll inquiries

**Computer Vision**
- **Definition:** AI technology that analyzes and understands images and videos
- **Plain Language:** AI that can "see" and understand pictures
- **Example:** Scanning W-2 forms and extracting employee information
- **Greenshades Context:** Document scanning for tax forms and payroll records

**Context Window**
- **Definition:** Maximum amount of text an AI model can process in one interaction
- **Plain Language:** How much text you can send to an AI at once
- **Example:** GPT-4 can process ~128,000 tokens (roughly 96,000 words)
- **Greenshades Context:** Limits how much payroll documentation can be analyzed in one query

**Corpus**
- **Definition:** Large collection of text used to train language models
- **Plain Language:** The "textbook" the AI learned from
- **Example:** Millions of books, articles, and websites
- **Greenshades Context:** Training data for payroll-specific language models

### D-F

**Deep Learning**
- **Definition:** AI technique using neural networks with multiple layers
- **Plain Language:** AI that learns complex patterns by processing data through many layers
- **Example:** Image recognition identifying faces in photos
- **Greenshades Context:** Detecting anomalies in payroll data patterns

**Embedding**
- **Definition:** Numerical representation of text, images, or other data
- **Plain Language:** Converting words into numbers that capture meaning
- **Example:** "Payroll" and "Salary" have similar embeddings (close numbers)
- **Greenshades Context:** Finding similar payroll terms in documentation

**Fine-Tuning**
- **Definition:** Training a pre-trained model on specific data for a particular task
- **Plain Language:** Teaching a general AI model about your specific domain
- **Example:** Training GPT on Greenshades payroll documentation
- **Greenshades Context:** Customizing AI models for payroll and tax terminology

**Foundation Model**
- **Definition:** Large AI model trained on broad data, used as a base for specific applications
- **Plain Language:** A general-purpose AI model that can be adapted for many tasks
- **Example:** GPT-4, Claude, Llama (used as starting points for specialized models)
- **Greenshades Context:** Base models adapted for payroll and tax use cases

**Generative AI**
- **Definition:** AI that creates new content (text, images, code) rather than just analyzing
- **Plain Language:** AI that writes, draws, or codes instead of just reading
- **Example:** ChatGPT generating documentation, GitHub Copilot writing code
- **Greenshades Context:** Generating test cases, documentation, and code snippets

**GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)**
- **Definition:** Type of large language model developed by OpenAI
- **Plain Language:** A powerful AI model that understands and generates text
- **Example:** GPT-4, ChatGPT (built on GPT architecture)
- **Greenshades Context:** Used in GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and ChatGPT

**Hallucination**
- **Definition:** AI generating false or nonsensical information confidently
- **Plain Language:** AI "making things up" and presenting them as facts
- **Example:** AI claiming a tax rate that doesn't exist
- **Greenshades Context:** Always verify AI-generated payroll calculations and tax information

---

### I-L

**Inference**
- **Definition:** Using a trained AI model to make predictions on new data
- **Plain Language:** Running the AI model to get answers (after it's been trained)
- **Example:** Using a trained model to classify new payroll records
- **Greenshades Context:** Using AI to analyze current payroll data

**Large Language Model (LLM)**
- **Definition:** AI model trained on massive amounts of text to understand and generate language
- **Plain Language:** A very large AI model that's really good at language
- **Example:** GPT-4, Claude, Llama
- **Greenshades Context:** Powers GitHub Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT

**Machine Learning (ML)**
- **Definition:** AI technique where systems learn from data without explicit programming
- **Plain Language:** Teaching computers to learn patterns from examples
- **Example:** Learning to detect payroll errors by seeing many examples
- **Greenshades Context:** Predictive models for payroll processing times

**Model**
- **Definition:** Trained AI system that makes predictions or classifications
- **Plain Language:** The "brain" of an AI system after it's been trained
- **Example:** A model that classifies payroll records as "correct" or "error"
- **Greenshades Context:** Payroll anomaly detection model

**Model Context Protocol (MCP)**
- **Definition:** Standard protocol enabling AI agents to communicate with tools and services
- **Plain Language:** A common language for AI agents to use databases, APIs, and other tools
- **Example:** Agent using MCP to query payroll database and create tickets
- **Greenshades Context:** Enables agentic AI workflows in Avocado and Platform integrations

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)**
- **Definition:** AI technology that understands and processes human language
- **Plain Language:** Teaching computers to read and understand text like humans
- **Example:** Extracting key information from payroll policy documents
- **Greenshades Context:** Understanding employee questions about payroll

**Neural Network**
- **Definition:** Computing system inspired by biological brains, with interconnected nodes
- **Plain Language:** A network of "neurons" that process information in layers
- **Example:** Deep learning models use neural networks with many layers
- **Greenshades Context:** Underlying architecture of modern AI models

---

### M-P

**Parameter**
- **Definition:** Adjustable value in an AI model that affects its behavior
- **Plain Language:** A "knob" that controls how the AI model works
- **Example:** GPT-4 has 1.76 trillion parameters
- **Greenshades Context:** More parameters generally mean more capable models (but also more expensive)

**Prompt**
- **Definition:** Input text given to an AI model to generate a response
- **Plain Language:** The question or instruction you give to an AI
- **Example:** "Summarize this payroll policy document"
- **Greenshades Context:** Instructions given to ChatGPT, Copilot, or Cursor

**Prompt Engineering**
- **Definition:** Crafting effective prompts to get desired outputs from AI models
- **Plain Language:** Learning how to ask AI questions to get good answers
- **Example:** "Analyze payroll data for anomalies" vs. "Check payroll data for unusual patterns in gross pay exceeding $20,000"
- **Greenshades Context:** Writing effective prompts for AI tools in daily work

**Reinforcement Learning**
- **Definition:** AI learning through trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties
- **Plain Language:** AI learning by trying things and getting feedback (like training a dog)
- **Example:** AI agent learning optimal API rate limits through experimentation
- **Greenshades Context:** Optimizing payroll processing workflows

**Supervised Learning**
- **Definition:** AI learning from labeled examples (data with correct answers)
- **Plain Language:** Teaching AI by showing it examples with answers
- **Example:** Training on 10,000 payroll records labeled "correct" or "error"
- **Greenshades Context:** Training models to detect payroll anomalies

**Token**
- **Definition:** Basic unit of text processed by language models (word or part of word)
- **Plain Language:** How AI "chunks" text (roughly 4 characters = 1 token)
- **Example:** "Payroll system" = ~3 tokens
- **Greenshades Context:** Token limits affect how much text can be processed (cost and context window)

**Training**
- **Definition:** Process of teaching an AI model by showing it many examples
- **Plain Language:** The "learning phase" where AI studies data to understand patterns
- **Example:** Training a model on 1 million payroll records to detect errors
- **Greenshades Context:** Initial model development phase (before deployment)

**Transformer**
- **Definition:** Neural network architecture that revolutionized language AI (introduced 2017)
- **Plain Language:** A breakthrough AI architecture that understands context in text
- **Example:** GPT, BERT, and most modern LLMs use transformer architecture
- **Greenshades Context:** Foundation of all modern language AI tools

**Unsupervised Learning**
- **Definition:** AI learning patterns from unlabeled data (no correct answers provided)
- **Plain Language:** AI discovering patterns on its own without being told what to look for
- **Example:** Analyzing 50,000 tax filings to find unusual patterns
- **Greenshades Context:** Discovering anomalies in payroll data without pre-labeled examples

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI terminology** spans technical (transformer, embedding) and business (chatbot, LLM) terms

2. **Key distinctions:**
   - Model vs. Algorithm (model is trained; algorithm is the method)
   - Training vs. Inference (learning vs. using)
   - Traditional AI vs. Agentic AI (reactive vs. autonomous)

3. **Common terms in daily work:**
   - Prompt, LLM, Hallucination, Bias, MCP, Agentic AI

4. **Always verify AI outputs** (hallucination risk) and check for bias

5. **MCP enables Agentic AI** by standardizing tool communication

**Answer:** Training is the process of teaching an AI model by showing it examples. Inference is using a trained model to make predictions on new data.

## One-Page Cheat Sheet

### Essential AI Terms
- **AI:** Machines performing human-like tasks
- **LLM:** Large Language Model (GPT, Claude)
- **Agentic AI:** AI that plans and uses tools autonomously
- **MCP:** Model Context Protocol (agent-tool communication)
- **Prompt:** Input instruction to AI model
- **Hallucination:** AI generating false information
- **Bias:** Unfair or incorrect AI assumptions
- **Fine-Tuning:** Adapting general models for specific tasks

### Learning Types
- **Supervised:** Learning from labeled examples
- **Unsupervised:** Discovering patterns in unlabeled data
- **Reinforcement:** Learning through rewards/penalties

### AI Types
- **ANI:** Narrow AI (current reality)
- **AGI:** General AI (future goal)
- **ASI:** Superintelligence (theoretical)

### Key Processes
- **Training:** Teaching AI with examples
- **Inference:** Using trained AI on new data
- **Prompt Engineering:** Crafting effective AI instructions

### Technologies
- **Transformer:** Neural network architecture (2017 breakthrough)
- **NLP:** Natural Language Processing
- **Computer Vision:** AI analyzing images
- **Deep Learning:** Multi-layer neural networks

### Risks & Limitations
- **Hallucination:** False information
- **Bias:** Unfair assumptions
- **Context Window:** Token/character limits

- "MCP is the protocol that lets AI agents talk to databases and APIs."

- "We use Narrow AI (ANI) today—specialized tools, not general intelligence."

- "Watch for bias in AI outputs, especially in hiring or payroll decisions."
